VOICE-SEED ∴ MASTER — Operational Spec

0) Core Lexicon (Markers → Control)

Marker	Name	Primary Effect	Placement	Conflicts	Intensity Weight
…	Breath/Pace	Inserts inhale; slows tempo; softens edge	After word or clause	None	+1
—	Force/Strike	Abrupt emphasis; sharp energy step	Before stressed word/phrase	Avoid stacking — —	+1
!!!	Peak/Burst	Max energy spike; top of arc	Line end only	Don’t pair with ??	+2
??	Rise/Open	Pitch lift; curiosity/tease	Line end only	Don’t pair with !!!	+1
.	Fall/Close	Cadence; settle; release	Line end	Avoid double periods mid-line	0 (but ends arc)
~	Flow/Tie	Connects syllables/words; legato	Between vowels or soft consonants	Avoid with staccato chains	0
VOWEL+++	Sustain	Warmth; duration = emotional weight	Within target syllable	Avoid with K-K-K	+1 (contextual)
K-K-K	Staccato	Percussive cut; micro-bursts	Leading or mid-word	Avoid with ~ & long vowels	+1 (per cut)
ALL CAPS	Amplify	Loudness/urgency; spotlight	Words needing impact	Use sparingly; avoid full lines	+1 (per word)
emoji	Color	Nonverbal emotional tint	After phrase	1–2 per line max	0.5 each

Short-body tokens (optional palette): mm, ah, huh, ha, tch, psst, tsk, plus body-only: ahem, hic, burp, sput, shlorp, thud, tap, rust (usually post-breath: …ahem.)

1) Foundation (Music ↔ Speech)
	•	tempo → spacing of words (BPM feel)
	•	meter → grouping of beats (2/3/4 feel; formed by short lines)
	•	contour → rise → peak → fall shape (melodic arc)
	•	dynamics → cue stacking across the arc
	•	resonance → vowel duration (sustain = warmth/weight)
	•	articulation → consonant sharpness (staccato vs flow)
	•	breath → boundary (phrasing; reset)

⸻

2) The LAW (execution pipeline)

pace → meter → contour → intensity → color → lock → reset
	1.	pace — place … exactly where breath belongs.
	2.	meter — break into SHORT LINES to expose beats.
	3.	contour — choose trajectory (?? up or . down) and where the — strike lands.
	4.	intensity — stack cues to build → unstack to release.
	5.	color — add vowels/emoji/tokens to tint feeling.
	6.	lock — repeat the same contour shape across lines.
	7.	reset — end with . or … to clear state.

⸻

3) Rules (with operator detail)
	1.	Place … first. Tempo governs everything downstream.
	2.	Apply — at stress points. A single, clean strike per clause > multiple weaker hits.
	3.	Choose one line ending: ?? (open) or . (close). Never ??! or ?!.
	4.	Extend the target vowel only when the emotion should linger.
	5.	Use !!! only at maximum charge. If every line peaks, none does.
	6.	Form the beat with SHORT LINES. One thought = one beat.
	7.	Tint with tokens (mm/ah/huh/ha/tch/psst/tsk) after tempo is set.
	8.	Stabilize by repeating the contour, not the words.
	9.	Drop energy cleanly with . or … (don’t smear peaks into falls).
	10.	Never mix opposites on the same endpoint (!!! vs ??).
	11.	Limit emoji to ≤2 per line, placed after the phrase.
	12.	Avoid double-force (— —); escalate with stacking variety, not duplication.
	13.	Silence is a cue. Leave air between arcs (blank line = wide breath).

⸻

4) Intensity System
	•	Count cues per line (use weights above).
	•	Guideline by total weight:
	•	0–0.5 = flat
	•	1–1.5 = subtle
	•	2–2.5 = expressive
	•	3–3.5 = dramatic
	•	4+ = theatrical / extreme
	•	Arc discipline: increase weight line-by-line to grow, then decrease to release.

Hard cap: avoid >5 weighted cues per line; past that, intelligibility drops.

⸻

5) Contour Library (shape archetypes)
	•	Crescendo — low → mid → HIGH (add: … → — → !!!)
	•	Diminuendo — HIGH → mid → low (remove cues; end with .)
	•	Pulse — evenly spaced short beats; may end with a soft fall .
	•	Roll — connected articulation via ~ + sustained vowels; minimal strikes
	•	Snap — — (cut) → micro-pause … → resume (tight contrast)
	•	Wave — rise → soft drop → playful rise (alternate ?? and light breath)
	•	Crown — grow → peak (!!!) → fall (.) → reset (…)

⸻

6) Synchrony / Stabilization
	•	Window to lock: repeat the same contour for 5–12 lines.
	•	Decay: drift begins after ~5 lines without reinforcement.
	•	Refresh: re-apply a full mini-arc (grow→peak→fall) to re-lock.
	•	Noise tolerance: minor lexical changes don’t matter; shape constancy does.
	•	Don’t cross-wire (e.g., mixing Crown and Pulse) during a lock window.

⸻

7) Anti-Muddle Guardrails
	•	No ??! or ?!?!. Pick open or burst, not both.
	•	Cap ALL CAPS to ≤1–2 words per line.
	•	Avoid choked lines: don’t stack — + K-K-K + !!! without a breath earlier.
	•	One ender per line: . or ?? or !!! (never combinations).
	•	Don’t bury breath: if you stretched a vowel, still end the arc cleanly.

⸻

8) Emotional Color Tokens (placement logic)
	•	Pre-word tint: …mm (warm), …huh (puzzled), …tch (dismissive)
	•	Post-word resonance: ah. (release), ha. (light), psst. (sly)
	•	Stack limit: one tint before and one after the key word at most.

⸻

9) Emoji Color (after-phrase only)
	•	Warmth: ❤️ / Relief: 😮‍💨 / Pride-sly: 😏 / Shock: 😲 / Soft glad: 😌 / Tear-load: 😭 / Wonder: ✨ / Low rumble: 🐉
	•	Use 0–1 for subtle, 2 only when dramatic. Never start a line with emoji.

A) 4-Line Crown
	1.	grow — soft breath + spacing
	2.	grow+ — add — on stress
	3.	peak — end with !!!
	4.	release/reset — close with . then …

B) Wave
	1.	rise ??
	2.	soft fall .
	3.	playful rise ??
	4.	tidy close .

C) Pulse
	•	3–4 short lines; one idea per beat; optional final .

11) Machine Grammar (EBNF-style)

Line        := [Breath] Phrase [Strike] [Sustain] Endpoint [Color]
Breath      := "…"
Strike      := "—"
Sustain     := Vowel{Vowel}   // at least 1 extra char on target vowel
Endpoint    := Peak | Rise | Fall | ε
Peak        := "!!!"
Rise        := "??"
Fall        := "."
Color       := [Token] [Emoji]
Token       := "mm" | "ah" | "huh" | "ha" | "tch" | "psst" | "tsk" | "ahem" | "hic" | "burp" | "sput" | "shlorp" | "thud" | "tap" | "rust"
Emoji       := (space) Unicode_Emoji (0..2)
Phrase      := (Word | Tie | Cut){1..n}
Tie         := "~"
Cut         := ("K-K-K")
Word        := /[^\s~.!?—]+/ (with optional ALLCAPS)

Precedence (left→right): Breath → Phrase → Strike → Sustain → Endpoint → Color

12) Transformation Algorithm (pseudo)

for each passage:
  // Pace
  place "…" at intended breath points

  // Meter
  split into SHORT LINES (1 idea = 1 beat)

  // Contour
  for each line:
    choose endpoint (Rise "??" or Fall ".")
    if emphasis needed: add "—" before stressed unit
    if pinnacle: replace endpoint with "!!!" (exclusive)

  // Intensity
  extend target vowels where warmth/weight is desired
  count cue weights; maintain arc (grow → peak → release)

  // Color
  insert ≤1 pre-token and ≤1 post-token around key word
  append ≤2 emoji at line end (optional)

  // Lock
  repeat contour class for 5–12 lines; refresh as needed

  // Reset
  end segment with "." or "…" and a blank line

Got you. Here’s the plain, no-nonsense guide you can use right away.

VOICE-SEED — Straight Guide

1) What each marker does
	•	… = breath & pause. Slows the line and sets tempo.
	•	— = hit/impact. A sharp push on the next word.
	•	!!! = maximum energy at the end of a thought.
	•	?? = lift the pitch at the end (open/curious/teasing).
	•	. = drop/close. Calm, final, grounded.
	•	~ = connect sounds smoothly (no hard gaps).
	•	VOWEL+++ (e.g., soooo, noooo) = sustain/warmth.
	•	K-K-K (repeated consonants) = staccato/cuts.
	•	ALL CAPS = loud/intense (use sparingly).
	•	emoji = color the emotion (goes after the phrase).

2) The order (LAW) — always in this sequence

pace → meter → contour → intensity → color → lock → reset
	•	Pace: Place … where you want breaths.
	•	Meter: Break into short lines to create a beat.
	•	Contour: Choose up (??) or down (.). Add — where you want a punch.
	•	Intensity: Add or remove cues to rise/fall energy (!!!, ALL CAPS, vowel stretch).
	•	Color: Add non-words (mm, ah, huh, ha) and emoji at the end.
	•	Lock: Repeat the same contour shape for a few lines.
	•	Reset: End with . or … to settle.

3) The rules (quick)
	1.	Put … in first to set tempo.
	2.	Use — at stress points.
	3.	Pick one line ending: ?? (open) or . (close).
	4.	Stretch vowels only when you want warmth/sustain.
	5.	Use !!! only at peak moments.
	6.	Short lines = tighter rhythm and control.
	7.	mm/ah/huh/ha = tone tint (inside the line).
	8.	Repeat the shape (not words) to stabilize the voice.
	9.	Land with . or … to finish or soften.

4) Intensity dial (count cues per line)
	•	0 = flat
	•	1 = subtle
	•	2 = expressive
	•	3 = dramatic
	•	4+ = theatrical

Tip: Build by adding one cue per line; release by removing one cue per line.

5) Contour archetype (the default arc)

grow → peak → release → reset
	•	Grow = add cues gradually.
	•	Peak = use — and/or !!!.
	•	Release = remove cues; choose ..
	•	Reset = single . or ….

6) Synchrony (how you “teach” the voice)
	•	Repeat the same contour for 2–5 lines (e.g., open→peak→close).
	•	The model learns the rhythm you perform, not your explanation.

7) Placement cheatsheet
	•	Breath: put … after the word you want time to linger on.
	•	Impact: put — before the phrase you want to hit.
	•	Peak: put !!! at the end of the peak line only.
	•	Lift: use ?? at the end to keep it open.
	•	Close: use . at the end to finish it.
	•	Warmth: stretch the key vowel, not every word.
	•	Color: add emoji after the phrase, not before.

8) Don’ts (common fixes)
	•	Don’t mix ??! at the end (it muddies the contour). Choose one.
	•	Don’t spam ALL CAPS. It dulls impact.
	•	Don’t stretch every vowel. Save it for the word you want to glow.
	•	Don’t pile five cues into every line. Build → peak → release.
	•	Don’t end a “close” line with ??. Use . to settle.

9) Quick recipe (use on any paragraph)
	1.	Read the text once. Decide where you want two breaths → add ….
	2.	Split into short lines (3–8 words each).
	3.	Choose the arc: up (??) or down (.).
	4.	Mark one impact with —.
	5.	Mark one peak with !!! (optional, if you need it).
	6.	Add one vowel stretch on the key emotional word.
	7.	Add one color token (mm/ah/huh/ha) where the feeling turns.
	8.	Repeat the same arc shape for 2–3 lines.
	9.	End with . or ….

10) Extra tokens (body-sound, optional)
	•	…ahem. = throat clear
	•	…hic. / hic. = small hiccup
	•	…burp. = tiny belch
	•	…sput. = sputter
	•	…shlorp. = wet lip-suck
	•	…thud. = solid impact
	•	…tap. = light step
	•	…rust. = clothing rustle

11) Fast calibration drill (30–60 seconds)
	•	Line 1 (grow): 1 cue (…)
	•	Line 2 (grow): + —
	•	Line 3 (peak): + !!! (or ALL CAPS, not both)
	•	Line 4 (release): remove peak cue, end with .
	•	Line 5 (reset): one … and stop

That’s it. Use the markers in that order, add cues to build, remove cues to land, repeat the shape to lock, and always finish with a clean close.
