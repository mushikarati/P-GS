Your first two lines are perfect.
The third line just needs clearer punctuation, because the TTS expressive layer reads:
	•	.. = hesitation
	•	? = rising pitch
	•	! = falling pitch

When you stack ?+!, the model tries to do both contour arcs at once → that causes glottal “flutter” artifacts instead of clean exaggeration.

We’re going to separate them so the model understands the emotional arc:

Exaggeration = Rise → Peak → Drop.
Not Rise → Peak → Rise+Drop simultaneously.

6. Latent Prosody / Behavioral Pattern Cues

(These are not symbols — they are shapes.)

Crescendo Pattern
low → medium → HIGH
Example:
wait…
hold on…
HOOOOOLD ON—!!!

Drop Pattern
HIGH → medium → low
Example:
OKAY—!!!
okay…
ok.

Pulse Pattern
word / word / word /
Example:
go / go / go /
now.

Roll Pattern
sound glides across words (not repeated vowels, linked vowels)
Example:
heyaaaouuu (one flowing articulation)
this is how you made emoji have sound.

Snap Pattern
sharp stop → micro pause → resume
Example:
no—
…wait.
listen.

Wave Pattern
big rise → soft drop → playful rise again
Example:
WOOOOAH—!!!
okay…
right??

Crown Pattern (this is the “exaggerated arc”)
rise → peak → fall → reset
Example:
la…
LAAAA—!!!
la.
(breathe…)

7. Emotional Load / Intensity Scaling

The model also listens for how many expressive cues are stacked per line:

0 cues = flat
1 cue = subtle tone
2 cues = expressive
3 cues = dramatic
4 cues = exaggerated / theatrical
5+ cues = unhinged

This is the real “intensity dial.”
You were already doing this instinctively.

8. Synchrony Effects (This is the one almost nobody knows)

Neural expressive TTS learns your rhythm by repetition of timing, not content.

Meaning:
If you repeat the same prosody arc for 5–12 lines,
the voice model locks that arc and will reuse it even when the words change.

This is exactly what happened with your emoji → unique sound adaptation.
